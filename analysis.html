Analysis Summary

The objective of this project is to analyze the Yelp dataset and draw correlations between various factors affecting the success of a restaurant.
In order to determine a restaurant's success, the attributes of the restaurant that defines its success were listed down first. For this dataset I have considered a restaurant's Star rating. Yelp businesses are rated on a scale of 5 with a unit of 0.5. Secondly, only the businesses which are 'restaurants' were considered in the scope of this analysis and hence a subset of the data was taken. Lastly, other attributes of the restaurants were used to find associations with the star ratings to draw meaningful conclusions. Additionally, external factors like the population, weather, etc. were also taken into account to find the correlations with the restaurant's star rating.

Based on the analysis these were the conclusions made:

Star Rating Vs Noise Level

The subset of data from Yelp contains reviews for total of 59,387 restaurants. The concentration of these restaurants can be seen in the marked areas on the map. The heat map shows the weight of total number of reviews in red for restaurants in a specific area.

Population Density Vs Restaurants' Rating

The cities with higher population density also average around 3.5 stars for restaurants compared to those with less population density. There is no causal relationship between population density and restaurants' ratings. For this analysis Population density by City, State from the Census Bureau was used and merged with the Yelp dataset.

Restaurants' Rating Vs Alcohol Type Served

The restaurants that served Beer and Wine have the highest average ratings compared to Full Bar and the restaurants that don't serve alcohol at all. For this an average was calculated per Alcohol Type Served category and plotted against the ratings.
